### 2. Inversion Thinking: Solve Problems by Working Backwards
- **Source:** https://creativitydrills.com/blog/inversion-thinking
- **Summary:** Inversion thinking approaches problems backwards by asking "how would I guarantee failure?" instead of "how do I succeed?" Borrowed from mathematician Carl Jacobi and popularized by Charlie Munger, it surfaces blind spots that forward reasoning misses and removes common failure modes before they occur.
- **Dimension:** inverse_thinking
- **Why this matters:** inverse_thinking is at 5.0 — this piece explains the cognitive asymmetry between success and failure knowledge, and why avoiding failure is often more reliable than predicting success.
